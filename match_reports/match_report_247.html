Q5: What happened next? (5 marks)

Fields' No 4 Miller had been quietly ticking along while wickets had gone at the other end. In the last 10 overs he went into overdrive, smashing Ron Weasley for a couple of huge maximums. Clark, Turley and Fernbank all chipped in to help him. Gupte bowled well and picked up the wicket of Clark with probably his worst ball - a long hop that he missed -  but Troja and Francis were a bit below par and the fielding began to flag. Even the normally solid Potty managed a comedy misfield through his legs. Fields finished on 213, which was a decent score on that wicket, but about 30 or 40 higher than they'd looked like getting at one stage. A hot and exhausted Village staggered off the field of play.

3/5 Failure to mention Mr Pitcher's expensive drop when Clark had just got to his 50. Should perhaps have mentioned that by this point the chat vortex had become a chat black hole of unprecedented proportions due to the heat.

Q9: Describe the "boring" middle overs (5 marks)

Potty and Josh Homme began to rebuild, then expanded. Pontin, who had previously been moaning about not playing "man cricket" at number 5, cashed in on some loose offerings from the change bowlers. Highlights included his trademark Sharapova forehand smash down the line, a top edged leg glance for six over square leg, and lots of insufferable shouty "banter". Johnny Bairstow made life easy for the scorers by making most of his runs on the other side of the wicket with a series of lofted cover drives interspersed with a few dicey edges. VCC remained up with, but never ahead of, the run rate. Pontin eventually went bowled for an excellent 76, his first significant score against Fields since...possibly ever.

4/5 Good detail, important to note the entirety of this bit was being livetweeted by #talktotroja to an increasingly captivated social media audience.
