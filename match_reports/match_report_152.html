The Sultans of Swing CC (201 for 9) beat The Village (200 all out) by 1 wicket
10 July 2010

 

Sometimes timing is everything.  As the Communique' went out for extra Villagers to take on The Sultans of Swing last Friday, after some Private Investigations, only by a matter of minutes did one Steve Loonstra miss his chance to make his debut for Village CC, and instead was put forward as a fill-in for our opposition.

Upon arrival at The Peter May Sports Centre we found ourselves scheduled to play on the smallest of grounds, and what’s more without any shade at all, which was particularly troublesome for C-Pitch who appeared to have consumed some Heavy Fuel overnight.  There were some calls from disgruntled Villagers from south of the river as to why we were playing a home game So Far Away, but with a green light and short boundaries our thoughts were with the absent Ollie Morgans.

After losing the toss (again) we were put in to bat on a pretty decent pitch.  The opening bowlers were bowling slow/medium and openers Bowman and Harty did their best to bat in an orthodox manner.  Bowman however got out bowled in the 3rd over attempting to launch a ball over the mid-wicket boundary, followed by Harty a few overs later, out bowled playing a pull shot (again).    

 

Ahmed Iiyas and Umar Butt came together for an entertaining partnership, which included plenty of lusty hitting from Butt and a four off the back of the bat from Ahmed.  Ahmed ended up getting caught on 27, closely followed by Umar for 36.  Enter Ali Moir who batted beautifully.  Ali showed great technique being stout in defense and equally able to dispatch the bad balls, of which he received quite a few.

 

Things were going well for the Village being about 4/150, when Steve Loonstra was brought into the attack.  His medium pacers were hitting the deck hard and we struggled to keep him out.  He had Ali caught behind for 56, Naz bowled for 8, Abrahams caught in the gully for 1, and Pratik bowled for a duck.  Matt Boa was out caught for 4, and meanwhile The Example was holding up the other end and was doing his best to get us to the 200 mark.  EK took a single to bring up to 200 off the 4th ball of a Loonstra over, and Loonstra cleaned up C-Pitch to end the innings on 200 even.   Loonstra, finishing with 5/25 off 8 overs.

 

The opposition openned with Loonstra and he batted really well, while wickets fell regularly at the other end.  KP stuck first with a plumb LBW shout, and Pratik bowled well to pick up two further wickets after a 50 run partnership.   C-Pitch then chimed in with a wicket, and the Sultans appeared to be struggling at 4/100.  However Loonstra was starting to increase his scoring rate and the total quickly got to 4/130, at which point KP threw the ball to Harty and said something to the effect “you got us into this mess, now show us your Latest Trick and get us out of it”**.  Harty picked up 2 quick wickets, but not the key wicket.  So KP pushed the field back to give Loonstra the single and focus the attack at the other end.  The strategy worked like a charm, with Pratik, Boa, EK all taking wickets, and the Sultans being 9 down with about 30 runs to get.

 

Local Hero Loonstra at this point farmed the strike and worked off the runs, bringing up his hundred with a six!  Two overs later the match had come Down to the (Water)Line, with the Sultans needing 7 runs to win with Loonstra on strike and 2 balls left in the over.  Next ball cut shot for 4.  So 3 runs required, one ball left in the over, Loonstra goes for a big cut shot from a lifting ball, thick edge, straight to fly slip, and a tough chance put down by Ali and the ball goes for 4.  Game over, Loonstra 113 not out, and victory to the Sultans of Swing.

 

Village Player of the Match: Pratik bowled well for his 3 wickets, Umar batted well for his 36, but Ali Moir is the Village Player of the Match with a stylish 56 runs.

Village Idiot: No question, KP for letting the opposition have Steve Loonstra
