Bright and sunny, loads of half naked sun bathers.  Some might say ideal conditions.

On the hottest day of the year, Young Village skipper Ilyas won the toss and elected to bat first on a slow South Park wicket.  Bilal and Donald got the Youngsters off to a solid start against Veteran Kiwi quicks Bowman and Hampton.  Hampton was fired up after a close LBW appeal against Bilal was turned down and a sharp chance behind the wicket was grassed the following ball.  The hungover Oldies struck soon after though, claiming the key wicket of the ever dangerous Donald off Bowman, thanks to a good diving forward (over the zimmer frame) catch.

Enter the greatest batsman to ever call himself a Villager in his trademark Red Shorts.  Despite having sipped pina coladas in some of Soho’s finest establishments until the early hours, Troja was in his typical stodgey early innings form, allowing the aggressive Bilal keep the run rate above 6 an over.  The two put on an 80 run partnership until Bilal was stumped by hungover stag Hindle off the bowling of Koder for a well compiled 49. 

Thus Troja accelerated, launching 2 sixes on his way to 50 retired, while Gupte and Ilyas came and went in relative quick succession trying to lift the run rate further.  And just when the Oldies felt a strong comeback was on the cards, the impressive Becker came to the crease and showed his class with a quick fire 40, ably supported with 15 from the ever improving Woodhouse, and stylish 14 from Pitcher. 

The innings finished after the allotted 35 overs with the Youngsters 5-219.  The score could have either been a lot more* or a lot less had the Oldies not dropped 10 catches, mostly off the bowling of Hampton.    

After a quick break Simson and White started the Oldies reply.  Becker and Woodhouse spearheaded the Youngsters attack and both bowled impressively, providing few scoring opportunities and extracting prodigious movement out of the pitch.  The scoreboard pressure became too much to opener White (9), who attempted to loft Woodhouse down the ground but only exceeded in skying the ball to mid off, 1-20 off 5 overs.  This brought the classy Littlejohns to the wicket, however his lack of match practice this year proved his undoing as Becker managed to find a gap between bat and pad to send him on his way for 2.

The former best Australian Village batsman joined Simson at the crease and Simson led the way with some elegant cover drives for 4.  Meanwhile Harty was looking shaky and none to pleased about the incessant sledging from the slip cordon - marvelous effort that.  Simson (20) fell soon after, and the Oldies were in trouble at 3-40.  Hindle joined Harty and was looking in great form, advancing down the wicket at every opportunity, however his stand didn't last long, presenting a simple catch to the Youngsters.  Bowman then arrived at the crease and looked good before Bilal slipped one through Bowman's brittle defenses, 5-45.  Hampton arrived at the wicket and was keen to continue his season objective of scoring 90% of his runs in sixes.  The crowd was somewhat stunned when he got off the mark first ball with a single, but déjà vu ensued as the following over the familiar pattern of 6, swish, out materialised, 6-52.

Koder joined Harty and the pair attempted to consolidate to avoid further embarrassment to the senior players of the club.  Harty finally started to see one ball instead of 3 and moved along to 20, and Koder demonstrated solid defense and the pair remained not out at drinks.    

The Youngsters, sensing victory was within their grasp, went for the kill bringing Troja and Ilyas into the attack.  However Harty was up to the challenge launching a Troja full toss into the adjacent postcode, and took 15 off the following Ilyas over.  Jitters went through the Youngsters camp and the experienced Gupte was brought into the attack.  Harty attempted to hit a straight 6 first ball and was lucky to survive as Troja uncharacteristically dropped a difficult chance.  Koder finally fell to a brilliant one-handed-diving-backwards catch from Becker, and skipper Francis came to the wicket and batted with composure as the two oldest Villagers tried to steal an unlikely victory.  However death bowler Gupte finally bowled Francis, leaving Harty stranded on 67 not out, and Old Village all out for 135.

Congratulations to Young Village for a strong all round effort and an emphatic 84 run victory.  The Old Village was certainly not at their best, but it was clear the better team won on the day.

Man of the Match: Bilal for a quick 49 and 2 key wickets.

* had Troja come back in score would have been 240+
