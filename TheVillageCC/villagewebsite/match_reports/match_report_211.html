Corfu 2012.

Early to mid-30s, blue skies and an astroturf pitch.  So, no swing, no variable bounce, no turn.  Just lots of bounce.

So, having slept till 1pm, 10* Villagers arrive after their most drunken night of the whole tour in quite a state.

Ground highlights included distant mountains across a bay, and a neighbouring pool and bar.  Oh, and a small patch of shade which was our sanctuary for the next few hours.

 

* 1 Villager had spent Thursday night being romanced by the local nurses in a clinic after losing badly at water-slide racing, so didn't drink much on the Friday night

The Village Goes Forth

After almost 10 years of hilarious 'cricket', in-fighting, failed coups and far too many pints to remember, The Village CC finally got its act together and went on its first official foreign tour.  It just wouldn't have been right and proper, nay, it wouldn't have been cricket if we hadn't gone and lost our first ever foreign tour match.  Not just lose, mind.  We were pwned in spectacular fashion.

After winning the toss, the honorary captain for the day, Bn, plumped for having a bit.  We all looked on with excitement* as Bomb and Harty strode out to the middle.  However, it was short lived as Steve was soon out to a sharp, low, diving catch in the slips - the sort of catch that only ever seems to happen to him (see "Parp! Innings").

Early Hope

Next in was The Example.  Between him and Alan, a partnership began to form and the rest of the players began to settle down on the side-lines, after commiserating the dismissed Steve.  It wasn’t easy going though, as the ball bounced up high on the AstroTurf pitch, which made it hard to get away.  The opening bowlers weren’t bad either.  One of them, a brute of a Pakistani, had pace and control.  He clearly knew what he was doing and changed his bowling any time the batsmen took up a new guard to try something different.  Despite this, we were soon on about 50 for 1.  A decent start, we all thought.  But Bomb had different ideas.  He decided that enough was enough and he wanted to see the back of our Great Leader.  So he promptly ran him out.

The Rate Slows

Out came the Parp!, and not for the last time that day.  He had no intention of staying out in that heat for long and said so – he’d rather be in the pool.  However, sadly for our run rate he wasn’t true to his word.  Throughout his innings, Parp! edged the ball behind numerous times, only to be dropped again, and again, and again – including by the same fielder who had caught Harty so expertly.  When this failed to get him out, there was some suicidal running between the wickets from Bomb and Parp!.  Though trying to tell who was attempting to run out who is difficult between the portly twins.  Despite all this crazy running and White’s best efforts at returning the ball back to its place of dwelling, the run rate was slipping down as Parp! tried, but failed, to get the ball off the square.  Thus Bomb took it upon himself to attempt to launch every ball into the next field – stating matter-of-factly that this is where the ball belongs.  It turned out he wasn’t quite right.  Then the inevitable happened and Alan was bowled for a very respectable 54.

Classic Village

If you are of a sensitive nature, may I suggest that you avert your gaze now?  Perhaps you could have a good old leer at your office hottie?  Spend some time staring absent mindedly out of the window.  Or, give your manager a heart-attack by doing some work.  For what follows is a sad tale; one that veteran Villagers now only too well.  Many of them wake in a cold sweat in the night, visions of those ghastly days flashing across their eyes.  Yes, after the valiant efforts of Bomb and Frank (31 not out – as ever), the Village went from roughly 110 for 2 to all out for 140.

The Procession

First to fall was Parp!.  Bowled shortly after having been left by his doppelgänger.  “Good riddance” said the rest of the Village as they spied a chance to up the rate.  But it wasn’t to be.  John Lucarotti came and went with barely a shot in anger.  Out strode the WLD, full of purpose.  He was determined to score this weekend!  Only Troja, who was then starting to look in good shape, went and did the wrong+ thing and ran him out.  Justice was soon served up on behalf of the Welshman though as Troj was trapped in front for 9 – a shock to all onlookers after his promising start.  Marrow was caught off of the bowling of a child and then the opening bowlers returned to polish off KP and Bn.

An Ugly Scorecard

We all trudged off the field to the cooing of our Greek tour organiser who was delighting in the beating his ‘countrymen’ were handing out to us.  Checking our scores was a mentally disturbing experience for all apart from the Bombus – but he’d soon be regretting ever visiting the Corfu mariner and its mosquitos.  He had, roughly, scored the same number of runs as the other ten batsmen, with only two of them getting into double figures.  Still, The Village is made of sterner stuff these days and, after a few ham sandwiches and gallons of water, we pulled up our socks and went out to bowl with a bounce in our step hoping for some early wickets.

HOME

There is a reason for my terminology earlier when talking about where Bomb hit the ball.  Yes, Alan hit some nice shots into the next field, but one large Pakistani (Ahmet, apparently) overruled him and showed us all where the ball’s HOME really is.  It wasn’t in the next field.  It wasn’t even over the parked boats on the back of trailers.  It wasn’t in the car park behind those, either.  No, it was on the banks of the water several hundred feet away.  An idyllic place, one would imagine.  I wouldn’t really know as the Villager sent to retrieve the ball hadn’t returned by the time we got on the plane to our home.  The ball also had a few nice holiday HOMEs elsewhere in the region.  It visited those quite frequently too.  In fact, it earned itself quite a lot of air miles in only a few overs.  Feeling sorry for us, Ahmet the Destroyer slipped his bat under his armpit and marched off after scoring his half century in about 15 balls.  Along with some decent contributions from those around him, the opposition only needed a handful more runs and they eked them out over the next few overs – toying with us like a cat playing with a half-dead mouse.  Even going so far as to continue playing with our ravaged bodies 15 runs over the winning line – the bastards.

The After Show Pool Party

So, with typical good grace, the Village congratulated the opposition and presented Ahmet with the MoM trophy.  Speaking no English whatsoever, he looked quite bemused at our attempts to recruit him to the Village right under the oppo captain’s nose.  After a cooling swim in the pool and a game of “throw the wobobo ball at your fellow Villager’s head”, we gathered some sun loungers and drank away the rest of the evening until the special bus (finally) arrived.  The mosquitos also got their fill.  JL’s back was quite a sight, but Bomb, with his anaphylaxis, was the biggest loser – spending the night in hospital without any travel insurance.  Clever.
