Changeable but hot when the sun came out

 Hangovers to the left, hangovers to the right, hangovers opening the batting and hangovers opening the bowling. It mattered not that the majority of Saturday’s Villagers had spent the previous evening, fuelled by £15 jugs of continental lager, watching the Qantas Socceroos in their futile late-night quest to put the Chilean national side to bed. The Village STREAK is back after a huge 111 run win over Strongroom in the idyllic Highgate Woods.

Batting first on a decent looking pitch, HK and JDM had a look at the opening bowlers before HK decided enough was enough and skied a catch to mid-on to leave him free to check out the sausage rolls on offer at the park café and compile the world’s most accurate scorebook. Nevertheless, JDM looked solid and was regularly seen knocking the ball over extra cover with ease as captured on video by square leg umpire N Thompson. Although the Village lost 2 wickets in 2 balls to Binay (including the returning Ben Hampton who got a ripper first up), de Mellow soon joined forces with the free-spirited L Becker, and the two began to take the game away from Strongroom.

By the time JDM departed for a well-constructed 75, the score had progressed to more than 160 and, with 8 overs to go, the Village had their sights set on 220. It was sometime around the 35th over when, nerves jangling but mind focused, Becker picked up a length ball and deposited it back over the bowlers head for a magnificent 6 to bring up his maiden 50. Strongroom looked concerned when Becker was dropped next ball following a regulation nick to the keeper, but it didn’t prove too costly as a mix up between Bilal and Becker soon resulted in the dashing number 5 returning to the hutch for a crucial 58.

Bilal kept the momentum up however, with a powerful 31 including some lusty leg-side hitting to leave the Village on 229 for 8 from their allocated 40 overs.

Tea was an efficient affair with pre-prepared sandwiches stuffed with a good selection of fillings including a delightful ham and avocado number and rice wrapped in vine-leaves.

Back out on the park, swing merchant M Boa was causing problems for the top order from the top end, whilst an unwashed N Thompson was doing his best to simultaneously ruin his ankle and decide if his previous evening’s tryst was really ‘amour’. As it turns out, ‘the one’ for Thompson was opposition captain Will who missed a quick and straight one to be sent back and leave the home team in trouble at 3 down for not very many. Boa’s opening spell left him with an impressive 3-17 off 6 but this would not be his final intervention of the day.

The only Strongroom batsman to offer any real resistance from the top order was opener “Jon” who took the Village bowling for ‘double’ after ‘double’ much to the amusement of the fielding side. The opener’s spell at the crease was ended by Naz whose middle overs brought him a superb 4-23 from 8 including Binay who was bowled the ball after he had returned a catch to the bowler off a no-ball.

Naz’s spell ended in a tale of two catches. Firstly, the safe hands of HK held on to a screamer at gully to send the Village into raptures possibly only rivalled by those exhibited by HK’s posh Notting Hill conquest from the night before. Secondly, with the game and a Michelle for Naz on offer, the batsman chipped the ball lightly to cover, Troja at slip yelled “that’s five” and M Boa decided to slowly palm the ball from a comfortable chest height down into the turf. No easier catch has ever been dropped.

The innings ended two overs later as HK’s mystery spin failed to beat the defences of Strongroom’s 10 and 11 but Bilal was successful in snaffling Riaz for an entertaining 20 courtesy of another good catch at gully by the mystery spinner.

All in all, an excellent performance from the Village as they look to REBUILD THE STREAK. No game next week due to the ‘international break’, but if the hunger and passion burns in the hearts of the players (more likely it was beer-induced heartburn) for the rest of the season like it did against Strongroom, there is no reason why the STREAK shouldn’t reach double figures this time.

The Woodman proved a popular venue for post-match drinks, food and Boa-baiting. Rumours of Chris Moyles also being in the pub remain unconfirmed but a good spot nonetheless.
