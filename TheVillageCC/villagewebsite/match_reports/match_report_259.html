The Village v Clapham In CC; Also known as the BOA cup

Venue: Regents Park

 Conditions

A comfortably muggy summer’s day in London where the clouds won most of the battle over the sun

 

Background

The Bradman-Gavaskar Trophy, The Frank Worrell Trophy, the Ranji Trophy, and fast forward to mid-May 2014 and we have a new name added to these elite list of trophies. The Boa Cup, which is an annual game between Village CC and Clapham In CC, named after Matthew Boa, a crucial member for both these clubs, and a committed and extremely enthusiastic club cricketer, also a serial holiday planner.

Pre match observations

As we were waiting for the game to start, on the pitch adjacent to us, an under-13 cricket game was nearing its final stages. Out of almost nowhere, one of the batsman and a cover fielder broke into fisticuffs, much to the surprise of a very middle class audience. The youth these days. I tell you!  Yes, I will be 30 soon, I am old dude.

Match Report

Inspired by this boisterous aggression on play, our Australian skipper chose to bat first. The openers De Mellow and Morgans were steady amid some accurate bowling. But soon Clapham In CC were rewarded for their perseverance and within an hour or of play, the score line read 70 for 4, with Village CC under some pressure.

But then the two Australian greats, Troja and Harty played patiently to start with, punishing the bad balls, and Clapham In CC were soon on the back foot. Troja was particularly attacking, making a very striking 70 replete with his trademark extra cover drives and late cuts. Harty made a notable 70 too, but his innings was halted after an attempted pull shot, resulted in a top edge hitting his chin leaving a slightly nasty mark. After 40 overs Village CC ended on an impressive 233 for 4

In response, Clapham In CC struggled from the word go losing two wickets in the fourth over. Village CC put in a strong all-round bowling performance with four bowlers taking two wickets each.

Gregory, Frecknall, Wildman and Clack could all have potentially got good scores but some aggressive bowling got the better of them. Frecknall stayed unbeaten however playing some good shots, but 233 proved a bit too much for Clapham In CC

Special mentions

Chris Pitcher took an absolute screamer at point. He will remember that catch that forever, and so will we.

Dev bowled a couple of memorable overs especially one which was a double wicket maiden

Boa unfortunately remained wicketless, but he got a photo op with the self-titled Boa Cup, and he had his moment in the sun spraying champagne (which was called the Fat Bastard) over Troja and a few others

Post match

Social Secretary Thommo (pronounced Toommo in a slightly Chinese accent) and Dev organized a very enjoyable barbeque and drinks session for all on Dev’s balcony overlooking Camden Lock. Special thanks to them, and also to the WAGs for gracing us with their presence. All in all a thoroughly enjoyable day.
