Village won the toss and elected to field

Our much anticipated match against Strongroom CC was billed by some as the toughest match of the season, and without KP leading the attack, a tough task ahead of us.

On arrival at the picturesque Highgate Wood, the heavens opened causing the start of play to be delayed and the match was reduced to 35 overs a side.  Strongroom described the pitch as ‘green and muddy’, showing a slightly different perspective from a Village team recently back from Wiltshire, whom thought the track was one of the better ones we had seen in recent times!  

Village won the toss and sent Strongroom in to face the music of Messers Boa and White.  The Strongroom openers showed great intent early with both players striking some lovely shots, however the damp outfield didn’t aid their cause.  Bomb, struggling with a chest infection, bowled a very good line and length, and with the help of an energetic Ahmed doing a magnificent job standing up to the stumps, Village managed to apply the brakes. 

Matty Boa struck first, trapping the classy Newkirk in front for 6.  And shortly after Village claimed wicket number 2 after a suicidal run ended in disaster for Strongroom.  Bun came into the attack and struggled to find his length early, and then settled into a terrific rhythm of tight stump to stump bowling, resulting in the amazing figures of 7 overs 1-12.  At the other end The Example bowled one of his best spells of the season, claiming 2 wickets bowled, conceding only 21 runs off his 7.  A feat made all the more amazing considering how slow the pitch was playing.

The Village ground fielding was generally excellent, with the only blemishes by way of a dropped sitter at point from an experienced left handed fixtures secretary, and a misjudged outfield chance from some guy with a walking frame.

In the end Village managed to restrict Strongroom to 6-114 off their 35 overs.  While the score was not huge, the quality of our opponents did shine through as the refused to throw away their wickets in search of quick runs.

Tarquin and star recruit (and Michael Clarke look-alike) Loonstra lead our reply.  Steady progress was made at a rate of around 2 an over against some quality medium pace bowling.  With the introduction of the change bowlers Loonstra started to open up with a lovely 4 followed by the only six of the day to deep midwicket. However later that over Loonstra skied a ball and was caught trying to repeat the dose, score about 1/40 off 18 overs. 

Enter the ‘Batsman of the Year’.  Morgans strode purposefully to the wicket and looked solid right from the start.  He and Tarquin began to build a partnership and increase the run rate, that is until left arm leg spinner Martin was brought into the attack.  Martin bowled 3 overs for 2 runs, and in his fourth nearly claimed the ‘Batsman of the Year’ to an inside edge that narrowly missed his leg stump. 

However from that point on Morgans put the foot on the accelerator and treated the crowd to a couple of classical drivers for 4.  Meanwhile Hindle was eventually out to the spin of Martin for a well compiled 29, score about 2/70 off 25 overs.  That brought Harty to the crease, and with 45 runs to get, he and Morgans set about the task.  The runs started to flow and one big over enabled the Village pair to put the result beyond doubt.  Morgans then celebrated in the next over with a fantastic reverse swipe for 3, and finally Harty hit the winning runs with a lofted drive for 3 on the second ball of the 32nd over.

Congratulations to the Village for a fine performance against some strong opponents and all round good guys.

Man of the Match goes to Morgans for an unbeaten 34 in good time on a slow difficult wicket.
